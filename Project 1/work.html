My Experience

Building Performance Lab
My internship at Building Performance Lab consists of a research project intended to reduce the greenhouse gas emissions from buildings in New York City. Throughout this internship I have played many distinct roles such as the role of Product Manager and Data Scientist. As a a Product Manager, I have worked to display demos to the team for new emerging technology that our team would be using and help everyone grasp a better understanding of why and when this technology would be used. As a Data Scientist, I have worked with Python to manage building data from Building Performance Lab's datasets. I have also worked to develop data structures and databases for large data sets. I have done research and collected public datasets related to energy features of NYC buildings and implemented methods for translating electronic building records into datasets to warehouse and analyze. I have done research and developed BAS standards to help analyze and predict energy consumption and carbon emissions. My team and I also use regression models and whole facility energy models to evaluate actual and predicted energy consumption. This includes using tools like USDOE Asset Score, EPA Portfolio Manager, OpenStudio, EnergyPlus, and custom analysis models developed by Building Performance Lab.
Major League Hacking
